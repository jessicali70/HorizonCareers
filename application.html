Application

Before you start any job, you will be required to fill out a job application form, which will give your employer factual information about you, from your employment history and educational background to qualifications and references and more. While your resume and cover letter may highlight mainly your strengths, job application forms help to corroborate your claims in them, but also give employers a full image of who you are as a job seeker. Although they are traditionally filled out in pen and paper format, they have been increasingly used online instead. Below are the basics of filling such forms out:

Application checklist

Personal

  1. Your Name
  2. Address, city, state, zip code
  3. Phone number
  4. Email Address
  5. Social security number
  6. Eligibility to work in the United States
  7. Felony convictions in the past five years (information about convictions varies based on state law)
  8. If under age eighteen:employee certificate

Education and Other Experience:

  1. School(s) attended, degrees, (projected) graduation date
  2. Certifications
  3. Skills and qualifications
  4. Grade Point Average (G.P.A.)
  5. Extracurricular activities, especially those where you held a leadership role
  6. Honor societies

Employment History

  1. Employer: Address, phone, email - Permission to contact the previous employer
  2. Supervisor
  3. Job title and responsibilities
  4. Salary
  5. Starting and ending dates of employment (month, day, and year)
  6. Reason for leaving

Job Position Information

  1. Title of the job you are applying for
  2. Hours/days available to work
  3. When you can start work

References

  1. Name
  2. Job title
  3. Company
  4. Address, phone, email

Be prepared before you start filling out these forms and gather the information above beforehand.

The application may also ask additional questions, in which you should do your best to answer truthfully and completely.
